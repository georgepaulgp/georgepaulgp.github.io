Projects

Bayesian energy use estimation

Research project · MSc dissertation

This research project focuses on estimating Energy Use Intensity (EUI) in office buildings using hierarchical Bayesian models. What I found fascinating early on was how uncertainty quantification could be used to support transparent, audit-aligned carbon assessments—especially in grey areas where traditional point estimates fall short. I’m currently exploring how to blend domain-informed priors with flexible likelihoods (e.g. Student-T) and apply posterior predictive checks to improve robustness. I'm also interested in how model structure can reflect real-world building heterogeneity across London and Manchester. The goal is to produce both accurate and interpretable outputs for environmental decision-making.

Bayesian neural network for supplier emissions

Personal project

In this self-driven project, I applied a Bayesian neural network to estimate carbon emissions from supplier data. What drew me in was the challenge of combining deep learning with probabilistic reasoning — especially in cases where data is incomplete or noisy. I was intrigued by how uncertainty could be harnessed not just for academic rigor, but for real operational risk scoring. I’m now expanding this project to include explainability via SHAP, and experimenting with hierarchical layers for sector-specific priors. I'm motivated by the question: how can we make black-box models more transparent and usable for sustainability teams?

Academic coursework

This project involved training a PPO agent in the Boxing-v5 environment from OpenAI Gym. What I enjoyed most was the feedback loop between policy updates and agent performance — seeing learning emerge through structured exploration. While this was a theoretical environment, it sparked my ongoing curiosity about applying RL in real-world energy systems, where control and optimisation under uncertainty are key.
