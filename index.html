Bayesian Energy Use Estimation

Research Project · MSc dissertation

This research project focuses on estimating Energy Use Intensity in office buildings using hierarchical Bayesian models. What I found fascinating early on was how uncertainty quantification could be used to support transparent, audit-aligned carbon assessments, especially in grey areas where traditional point estimates fall short. I’m currently exploring how to blend domain-informed priors with flexible likelihoods and apply posterior predictive checks to improve robustness. I'm also interested in how model structure can reflect real-world building heterogeneity across. The goal is to produce both accurate and interpretable outputs for environmental decision-making.

2025

Intelligent Carbon Risk Management

Proposal and Research Plan

This project defines a traceable Bayesian framework for embedded carbon emissions in supply chains. The model estimates confidence-weighted emissions scores, identifies high-risk suppliers, and supports audit-ready dashboards for emissions benchmarking. I’m currently designing the prototype to integrate structured and semi-structured data, with plans to validate traceability through scenario analysis and simulated audits. The long-term goal is a deployable decision tool that aligns with compliance rules like CA SB 253 and supports supplier responsibility reporting.
